THE SONG OF ICE AND FIRE


AUTHOR: GEORGE R.R. MARTIN

RATINGS: 4.63 STARS

ABOUT BOOK:

George R. R. Martin's A Song of Ice and Fire series has become, in many ways, the gold standard for modern epic fantasy.
Martin—dubbed the "American Tolkien" by Time magazine—has created a world that is as rich and vital as any piece of historical fiction, set in an age of knights and chivalry and filled with a plethora of fascinating, multidimensional characters that you love, hate to love, or love to hate as they struggle for control of a divided kingdom.
It is this very vitality that has led it to be adapted as the HBO miniseries “Game of Thrones.”

The series includes the following novels:

A GAME OF THRONES
A CLASH OF KINGS
A STORM OF SWORDS
A FEAST FOR CROWS

Martin's present home is Santa Fe, New Mexico. He is a member of Science Fiction & Fantasy Writers of America (he was South-Central Regional Director 1977-1979, and Vice President 1996-1998), and of Writers' Guild of America, West.

Outside the towering stone walls that surround the Glade is a limitless, ever-changing maze. It’s the only way out—and no one’s ever made it through alive.

Everything is going to change.

Then a girl arrives. The first girl ever. And the message she delivers is terrifying.

2 STATES


AUTHOR: CHETAN BHAGAT

RATINGS: 3.43 STARS

ABOUT BOOK:

Love marriages around the world are simple: Boy loves girl. Girl loves boy. They get married. In India, there are a few more steps: Boy loves Girl. Girl loves Boy. Girl's family has to love boy. Boy's family has to love girl. Girl's Family has to love Boy's Family. Boy's family has to love girl's family. Girl and Boy still love each other. They get married.

Welcome to 2 States, a story about Krish and Ananya. They are from two different states of India, deeply in love and want to get married. Of course, their parents don't agrees. To convert their love story into a love marriage, the couple have a tough battle in front of them. For it is easy to fight and rebel, but it is much harder to convince. Will they make it?

From the author of blockbusters Five Point Someone, One Night @ the Call Center and The 3 Mistakes of My Life, comes another witty tale about inter-community marriages in modern india.

ABOUT THE AUTHOR:

Chetan Bhagat writes op-ed columns for English and Hindi newspapers, including Times of India and Dainik Bhaskar, focusing on youth and issues based on national development.
Bhagat is also a motivational speaker and has given talks in leading MNCs and other institutions. He quit his international investment banking career in 2009, to devote his entire time to writing. In 2008, The New York Times called Bhagat "the biggest selling English language novelist in India’s history".
Bhagat, a graduate of Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Delhi and IIM Ahmedabad, is seen more as a youth icon than as an author.

REVIEWS:

1.One thing I could say after watching 'Gone Girl' is I am becoming a David Fincher fan and looking forward to watching his other movies too. This film is a suspense-thriller in which a lady psychopath tries to take revenge in wrath. The movie was engrossing and attracted me as moth gets attracted to light.
It definitely had twists and turns which are the core of suspense movies, the storytelling was amazing. First, it makes you think that something has happened, then you understand what actually happened and the story doesn't end up there, it becomes more interesting and keeps the viewers on their nerves as it gets difficult for them to predict the outcome.
Although the storyline was pretty much interesting, the climax was a bit disappointing for me, it could have been better but they somehow tried to make it neutral. Nick(male protagonist) ends up with his wife Amy(female protagonist) at the end after all that stuff that happened was getting difficult for me to digest. Overall, it's a pretty good movie that will take you to David Fincher's different world for two hours, Don't miss a chance to get into this world!!


2. I would rate the novel as mediocre. Although i still got movie to watch, but as far as novel is concerned it's bit of a overhyped. Sociopathy was interesting so was the story. Characters were different yet believable. But the overhyped rating and reviews got me so excited about the novel that when i finally read it....it turn out as average. I bet if someone read this book without any prior prejudice set by ratings and reviews, that person will find it amazing. Personally ( my individual opinion) i found the story a bit stretched and Amy's confession quite unreasonable. Suspense and twist could be much better. If you want to read the book...go ahead by all means. But, do not set any type of prejudice or expectation and It will turn out to be an interesting read.
